About Me

I second year Computer Science student at Reichman University. Prior to that, I was an English teacher at NewSchool as well as a hobbyist game developer. In the military, I was a combat soldier in the IDF.
In my free time, I enjoy studying and learning about Machine Learning and Computer Vision; I sincerely hope to contribute to the field in the future. I am currently looking for a research internship or a job which will give me "hands-on" experience in the field.

My Journey in Game Development

As computer graphics is intertwined with game development, I have a strong interest in the field and have a relatively in-depth understanding of the subject. I've created small-scale shaders for my games and have a good understanding of the rendering pipeline.
As a hobbyist game developer, I've worked on multiple games, although none of my original games have been published; games I've worked on include a 2D top-down survival shooter, a space shooter, and a maze roguelike. My most recent game, "Dragon Snake", is a take on the classic game Snake and is fully playable and downloadable on itch.io (link above). It was mostly created to learn about the Godot game engine.

Currently, in my non-existent free time I am learning Unreal Engine 5, a well-known game engine which is used in the industry (not just in games! Also movies, for example). My goal is to break into 3D game development, and I believe that Unreal Engine 5 is the best way to do so. I also wish to gain more understanding and experience working with 3D models and animations, using Blender.
